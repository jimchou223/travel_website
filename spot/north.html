Taipei -- National Palace Museum

Taipei is a friendly city with its blend of Chinese culture with a curious of Japanese, Southeast Asian and American influences. Thanks to advances in economic development and the robust transportation systems, important international conferences and exhibitions are frequently held here in Taipei, drawing overseas travelers to visit, do business, stay and study in Taipei City. Its ethnic fusion has styled Taipei to become a melting pot of cultural diversity.

Overiew

Welcome to the world's largest and finest complete collection of Chinese traditional art, this vast palace covers all kinds of treasures in painting, calligraphy, statuary, bronzes, lacquerware, ceramics, jade and religious objects. Some of the most popular items, such as the famous Jadeite Cabbage, are always on display. The historical range at this museum is truly outstanding. Even within a single category, such as ceramics, pieces range over multiple dynasties, and even back to Neolithic times. If you are interested in history, one day is not enough for you to explore!

Inforamtion

The museum offers free guided tours in English at 10am and 3pm (book online or register in person about 20 minutes before, limited to 30 people). If you prefer to move about at your own pace, try an English headphone guide (NT$150). There is also a museum gift shop worth exploring.

Traffic

From MRT Red Line to Shilin station, walk outside the station and board Bus R30, fare NT$15, payable by cash or Easycard. Busses on the MRT station side of the road head east towards the museum. Busses generally leave every 30 mins on weekdays until 7PM and 15 mins on weekends until 6:55PM. To visit Shilin Night Market after the museum, take R30 back to the city, but stay on after MRT Shilin until the night market MRT Jiantan station. Additional busses are available after hours on Friday and Saturday evenings. Bus BR20 also travels from the museum to Brown Line 1 Dazhi station, however, Shilin is recommended for speed and convenience.
